Button weight lab

The current buttons, then three progressively lighter treatments — same geometry (36px · 3px radius · mono label), different weight. Hover each to feel its interaction. Pick one (or a blend) and I'll apply it to _shared/asterlogos.css, updating every prototype.

↓ hover the buttons — the difference is mostly in the border weight, the rest shadow, and the hover

Now

Current

Full-ink border, hard letterpress on every button, hover flips the whole thing to near-black. Bold — reads a touch industrial against paper.

Option A

Softer ink

Border drops to the hairline --rule; rest shadow becomes a whisper; hover inks the edge and lifts, instead of inverting to black. Primary uses --ink-soft, not pure black. Keeps the letterpress DNA, loses the heaviness.

Option B

Flat quiet

Quiet buttons carry no shadow at rest — just a hairline on bare paper; the letterpress lift appears only on hover. Calmest at rest; the press still feels physical.

Option C

Text-forward

Quiet actions become bare mono labels that ink on hover — only the committing action stays a filled button. Most e-reader-like; risks under-signalling that quiet items are clickable.

My pick: A (Softer ink) — it's the smallest departure that removes the heaviness while keeping the letterpress signature the whole aesthetic is built on. B if you want it even quieter at rest.
