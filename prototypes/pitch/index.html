What it is

Asterlogos builds interactive editions of great books. Each one turns a book's own structure into a place you enter and teach from — freeze on any moment, follow the discussion wherever it goes, and return without losing your thread. It's a finished edition with the book's name on it, not presentation software.

What a slide can't do

It keeps the whole poem in view

Slides replace each other and destroy "where are we." Here the whole cosmos stays; you move within it. Your class always sees where this moment sits.

It moves where the seminar moves

A reader asks about the underworld — go there and come straight back, the map intact. No deck reorders itself mid-discussion.

The picture is the argument

The layout isn't decoration. Gods above, the sea between, the dead below — moving through the form is teaching the structure.

For centuries, to teach a cosmos you built one you could stand inside.

The armillary sphere, the orrery, the memory theatre — instruments that made a structure something you could enter. They gave way to the printed page and the projected slide because those were cheaper, not better. Asterlogos is that instrument returning, now that it's finally affordable to build one.
